    Who we are


    More than just a bike shop



    Are you looking for an affordable, green mode of transportation? Then we can probably help you out. We've got a constant supply of bicycles being refurbished by our awesome Cycologists. Our bikes have an average price of $75 and we accept cash, debit, or credit card. We also sell helmets and Cycology branded goods, both of which we highly recommend.

    Cycology strives to achieve a triple bottom line (people, profit, planet) by providing training and employment in the field of bike mechanics to people who are economically disadvantaged, while at the same time diverting bikes destined for scrap and landfill sites

    Cycology Bikes was established in September 2017. A modern open concept location in Uptown Waterloo, Cycology has helped Waterloo Region’s cyclist population grow by one bicycle at a time.



    Donating a bike to Cycology helps someone else in our community afford one. Through repairing the bike community members can learn & share skills together. Using bicycles for transportation can help reduce our dependence on non-renewable fossil fuels which, when burned, contribute to a host of environmental problems.

    Back in 2014, our Cycologists on over 5,050 public bicycles! Waterloo citizens donated to us get a new lease on life – using things longer, or again, keeps us from needlessly consuming more resources. We are dedicated to green living by providing information and hands-on experience on working with not just refurbished bikes but parts and tools. We provide great service and the skills to maintain and repair your own bikes.


    Since opening in 2017, Cycology saved 940 bicycles from going to landfill!



    When the bicycle has finally reached the end of its life, we disassemble it to save any useable components to fix other bikes. Any parts that are not usable get recycled. We work hard to make sure as little waste is produced as possible.

    The Gaia

    The bike’s construction requires 90% less energy than regular bike frames. They are also designed to be rust-resistant, more shock-absorbent, and even less jarring on the prostate and spine. The Gaia is made up of paper, metal, rubber, plastic, and polystyrene that has all been recycled. Kraft paper makes up the bulk of the structure, but it’s not as flimsy as it sounds. The paper is pressed and compacted into individual panels that have been built to withstand a great amount of force. By combining hundreds of these panels together and adding a simple coat of recycled polystyrene paint, this paper bike is completely waterproof and ready to handle any commute.”

    The Gaia is named after the mother earth deity important for the Minoan civilization of ancient Crete, and referencing the Gaia hypothesis. In this name we show our dedication towards maintaining a greener world and environmental sustainability.
